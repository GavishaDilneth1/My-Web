Home Page : Introduction to Robotics and its Applications



Robotics, a field that blends computer science, engineering, and design, focuses on creating and utilizing machines called robots to perform tasks, often mimicking or replacing human actions. These machines are programmed to assist humans in various ways, ranging from simple repetitive tasks to complex operations requiring specialized skills and knowledge.


Robotics applications are diverse and span industries like manufacturing, healthcare, agriculture, and logistics, highlighting its growing importance in modern society.







Robots in Industry : Key advancements in Industries Automation



Key advancements in industrial automation include the increasing adoption of collaborative robots (cobots) that work alongside humans, enhancing both efficiency and safety. Artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) are also driving advancements, enabling systems to learn from data, adapt to changing environments, and make more informed decisions.


The Internet of Things (IoT) allows for real-time data collection and analysis, providing insights into process performance and enabling predictive maintenance. These advancements, along with digital transformation initiatives, are transforming industrial automation into a more flexible, efficient, and data-driven field.







Humanoid Robots : Overview of Humanoid Robots and Their capabilities



Humanoids are robots that resemble and behave like the human body. They’re not only made to mimic our body form but also to act in areas originally meant for humans.

Humanoid robots are available in diverse configurations tailored to their locomotion and intended application. The major categories include leg‐based humanoids well adapted to more dynamic environments and wheel‐based robots that are optimized for flat, stable ground.
